Definition

A parametrized curve $\vec r(t)$ is called smooth provided \[\vec r'(t) \neq \vec 0\] for all $t$. A smooth curve has a well-defined unit tangent vector \[\vec T(t) = \frac{\vec r'(t)}{|\vec r'(t)|}\]

Arc Length

To measure the arc length of a path in space, we would like to just "straighten it out" (without stretching) and use the distance formula but this is difficult, mathematically.

Arc Length Formula

So a reasonable approximation is

\[\sum_{i=1}^n |\vec r(a + i \Delta t) - \vec r(a+(i-1)\Delta t)|\]
\[ = \sum_{i=1}^n \left|\frac{\vec r(a + i \Delta t) - \vec r(a+(i-1)\Delta t)}{\Delta t}\right| \Delta t \]
\[ \longrightarrow \int_a^b |\vec r'(t)|\,dt\]

Motion

Position, Velocity, Acceleration

Suppose the position of a particle in space at time $t$ is given by $\vec r(t)$. Its derivative $\vec r'(t) = \vec v(t)$ is called the velocity, and its second derivative $\vec r''(t) = \vec a(t)$ is the acceleration. \[\vec a(t) = \vec v'(t) = \vec r''(t) \]
Speed is the magnitude of velocity $v = |\vec v(t)|$.
You are likely familiar with Newton's Second Law of motion \[\vec F = m \,\vec a.\]
