1-minute review

Double Integrals

\[ \iint\limits_{\mathcal R} f\,dA =\int_a^b \int_{g(x)}^{h(x)} f(x,y)\,dy\,dx. \]

Tripl Integrals

\[ \iiint\limits_{\mathcal E} f\,dV =\int_a^b \int_{g(x)}^{h(x)} \int_{j(x,y)}^{k(x,y)}f(x,y,z)\,dz\,dy\,dx. \]

Properties
  • $\displaystyle \iint_{\mathcal R} (f + g)\,dA = \iint_{\mathcal R} f\,dA + \iint_{\mathcal R} g\,dA$
  • $\displaystyle \iint_{\mathcal R} cf\,dA = c\iint_{\mathcal R} f\,dA$
  • $\displaystyle \iint_{\mathcal R} c\,dA = c\operatorname{Area}(\mathcal R)$.

Order of Integration

Example

Let $\mathcal E$ be the solid region in the first octant of $\RR^3$ bound by the surfaces $x + z = 1$ and $y = x^2$. Write the triple integral \[\iiint\limits_{\mathcal E} f\,dV\] as an iterated integral at least 2 ways.

Polar Coordinates

Formulas

A point in $\mathbb{R}^2$ can be described in rectangular coordinates $(x,y)$ with $-\infty < x,y < \infty$ or polar coordinates $(r, \theta)$ with $0 \leq r < \infty, 0 \leq \theta < 2\pi$. \[ \begin{align*} x &= r \cos \theta \\ y &= r \sin \theta \end{align*}\]
